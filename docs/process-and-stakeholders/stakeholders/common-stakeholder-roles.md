# Common Stakeholder Roles

*Part of: [Compliance Process and Stakeholders](../index.md)*

---

### Executive Leadership Roles

#### **Board of Directors**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Strategic oversight of compliance program
  - Approval of compliance policies and resource allocation
  - Review of compliance status reports
  - Risk appetite determination
- **Time Commitment**: Quarterly reviews, major milestone approvals
- **Key Interactions**: Compliance Officers, Audit Committee, External Auditors

#### **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Ultimate accountability for compliance
  - Tone-at-the-top setting
  - Resource allocation approval
  - External stakeholder communication
- **Time Commitment**: Monthly compliance reviews, critical decision points
- **Key Interactions**: All C-suite, Board, Regulators

#### **Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) / Chief Privacy Officer (CPO)**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Overall compliance program leadership
  - Policy development and oversight
  - Regulatory relationship management
  - Compliance reporting to Board and CEO
  - Incident escalation management
- **Time Commitment**: Full-time dedicated role
- **Key Interactions**: All stakeholders, primary interface for compliance

#### **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Security control implementation
  - Risk assessment oversight
  - Security incident management
  - Technical compliance requirements
- **Time Commitment**: Full-time, 30-50% on compliance
- **Key Interactions**: IT, Compliance, External Auditors

### Operational Roles

#### **Compliance Team**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Day-to-day compliance operations
  - Policy documentation and updates
  - Training coordination
  - Control testing and monitoring
  - Evidence collection and management
- **Team Size**: Varies by organization (1-20+ staff)
- **Key Skills**: Regulatory knowledge, project management, audit

#### **Legal Department**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Regulatory interpretation
  - Legal risk assessment
  - Contract review (vendor compliance)
  - Regulatory filing support
  - Litigation/enforcement response
- **Time Commitment**: Variable, 10-30% on compliance
- **Key Interactions**: Compliance, External Counsel, Regulators

#### **Internal Audit**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Independent compliance assessments
  - Control effectiveness testing
  - Audit report preparation
  - Risk identification
- **Time Commitment**: Variable, dedicated audit cycles
- **Key Interactions**: Compliance, Management, Audit Committee

#### **IT Department**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Technical control implementation
  - System configuration and maintenance
  - Security monitoring
  - Data management infrastructure
  - Technology vendor management
- **Team Size**: Variable, cross-functional involvement
- **Key Interactions**: Compliance, CISO, Vendors

#### **Human Resources**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Training program delivery
  - Background checks and screening
  - Policy enforcement (employee relations)
  - Competency management
  - Compliance culture promotion
- **Time Commitment**: Variable, 10-20% on compliance
- **Key Interactions**: Compliance, Department Managers, Employees

#### **Department Managers**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Control implementation within departments
  - Team training and awareness
  - Incident reporting
  - Local policy enforcement
  - Compliance integration into workflows
- **Time Commitment**: Ongoing, 5-15% on compliance activities
- **Key Interactions**: Compliance, Their Teams, HR

#### **Risk Management**
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Enterprise risk assessment
  - Risk register maintenance
  - Risk mitigation strategy
  - Risk reporting to leadership
- **Time Commitment**: Ongoing, significant overlap with compliance
- **Key Interactions**: Compliance, Internal Audit, Executive Leadership

### External Stakeholders

#### **External Auditors**

**Primary Role**: Independent third-party assessment of compliance posture

**Types**:
- **CPA Firms** (for SOC 2): Big Four (Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PwC) or specialized firms
- **ISO Certification Bodies**: Accredited registrars (BSI, DNV, TÜV, SGS, etc.)
- **Specialized Auditors**: Privacy auditors, medical device auditors, GxP auditors

**Typical Engagement Timeline**:
- **Initial Selection** (Month 0): RFP process, firm selection, engagement letter
- **Planning Meeting** (4-6 weeks before audit): Scope review, schedule coordination, evidence requests
- **Document Review/Stage 1** (2-4 weeks before on-site): Remote review of policies, procedures, evidence
- **On-Site Audit/Stage 2** (1-2 weeks): Interviews, walkthroughs, testing
- **Draft Report** (2-3 weeks after audit): Findings review, management response period
- **Final Report** (4-6 weeks after audit): Certification/attestation issuance
- **Surveillance Audits** (annually for ISO): Shorter audits to verify ongoing compliance
- **Recertification** (3 years for ISO): Full audit cycle

**What They Do**:
- Review policies, procedures, and documentation
- Interview staff at all levels
- Test control effectiveness through sampling
- Inspect physical and technical safeguards
- Review evidence (logs, tickets, records, training records)
- Assess risk management processes
- Verify corrective actions from previous audits
- Issue audit reports with findings (observations, non-conformities, opportunities for improvement)

**What They Need From You**:
- **Pre-Audit**:
  - Evidence request list (PBC - Provided By Client)
  - Organizational charts
  - Policy and procedure documentation
  - Risk assessments
  - Previous audit reports
  - Access to systems for testing
- **During Audit**:
  - Staff availability for interviews
  - System access for walkthroughs
  - Real-time evidence (system demonstrations)
  - Workspace for auditors
- **Post-Audit**:
  - Management responses to findings
  - Corrective action plans
  - Evidence of remediation

**Cost Expectations**:
- **SOC 2 Type I**: $15,000-$50,000
- **SOC 2 Type II**: $25,000-$80,000+
- **ISO 27001**: $20,000-$60,000 (certification), $5,000-$15,000 (annual surveillance)
- **ISO 13485**: $30,000-$80,000+ (depends on device scope)
- **HIPAA External Assessment**: $25,000-$100,000+ (voluntary)

**Key Interactions**:
- **Primary Contact**: Chief Compliance Officer or designated audit coordinator
- **Regular Meetings**: Opening meeting, daily debriefs, closing meeting
- **Frequency**: Weekly status updates during active audit
- **Documentation**: Formal audit requests, management responses, corrective action tracking

**Red Flags They Look For**:
- Inconsistent documentation
- Lack of evidence for key controls
- Control failures without corrective actions
- Poor security hygiene (default passwords, open access)
- Incomplete risk assessments
- Training gaps
- Incidents not properly documented/resolved

#### **Regulatory Authorities**

**Primary Role**: Government agencies that establish, interpret, and enforce compliance requirements

**By Framework**:

##### **FDA (Food and Drug Administration)** - US Medical Devices & Pharmaceuticals

**What They Do**:
- Review premarket submissions (510(k), PMA, De Novo)
- Conduct facility inspections (announced and unannounced)
- Review adverse event reports (MDRs, MedWatch)
- Issue warning letters and consent decrees
- Approve or reject marketing applications
- Monitor post-market surveillance

**Interaction Timeline**:
- **Premarket Review**: 3-12+ months depending on submission type
- **Routine Inspections**: Every 2-5 years (risk-based)
- **For-Cause Inspections**: Triggered by complaints, adverse events, or recalls
- **Adverse Event Reporting**: 5-30 days depending on severity

**What They Require**:
- Premarket submissions with validation evidence
- QMS documentation (21 CFR 820 or ISO 13485)
- Design history files (DHF)
- Device master records (DMR)
- Device history records (DHR)
- Post-market surveillance data
- Adverse event reports
- FDA registration and device listing

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Warning letters (public)
- Consent decrees (court-enforced agreements)
- Product recalls or seizures
- Import/export restrictions
- Criminal prosecution (in severe cases)
- Civil monetary penalties

**Key Contacts**:
- Regulatory Affairs team (primary)
- Quality Assurance
- CEO (for serious matters)
- Legal counsel

---

##### **HHS Office for Civil Rights (OCR)** - HIPAA Enforcement

**What They Do**:
- Investigate HIPAA complaints
- Conduct compliance reviews (audits)
- Enforce HIPAA Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules
- Impose civil monetary penalties
- Provide technical assistance and guidance

**Interaction Timeline**:
- **Complaint Investigation**: 30-180 days typical
- **Breach Reporting**: Within 60 days of discovery (500+ individuals), annually for smaller breaches
- **Compliance Reviews**: Periodic, risk-based selection
- **Resolution**: Can take months to years

**What They Require**:
- Response to complaints (30 days typically)
- Documentation of policies, procedures, risk assessments
- Breach investigation reports
- Evidence of corrective actions
- Training records
- Business Associate Agreements

**Penalties**:
- Tier 1: $100-$50,000 per violation (unknowing)
- Tier 2: $1,000-$50,000 per violation (reasonable cause)
- Tier 3: $10,000-$50,000 per violation (willful neglect, corrected)
- Tier 4: $50,000 per violation (willful neglect, not corrected)
- Annual maximum: $1.5 million per violation type

**Key Contacts**:
- HIPAA Privacy Officer
- HIPAA Security Officer
- Legal counsel
- CEO (for major breaches/investigations)

##### **Data Protection Authorities (DPAs)** - GDPR Enforcement

**Examples**: ICO (UK), CNIL (France), BfDI (Germany), EDPB (EU-wide coordination)

**What They Do**:
- Investigate data protection complaints
- Conduct audits and inspections
- Issue guidance and opinions
- Impose administrative fines
- Order corrective measures (stop processing, delete data)
- Approve Binding Corporate Rules and codes of conduct

**Interaction Timeline**:
- **Breach Notification**: Within 72 hours of awareness
- **Data Subject Complaint Response**: 1-3 months
- **Investigation**: 3-12+ months
- **Appeals**: Can add 6-18 months

**What They Require**:
- Records of Processing Activities (RoPA)
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)
- Data Processing Agreements with processors
- Evidence of lawful basis for processing
- Data subject rights response procedures
- DPO contact information (if applicable)
- International transfer safeguards

**Penalties**:
- Lower tier: Up to €10M or 2% of global annual turnover
- Upper tier: Up to €20M or 4% of global annual turnover (whichever is higher)
- Recent major fines: Amazon (€746M), Meta (€1.2B), Google (€90M)

**Key Contacts**:
- Data Protection Officer (DPO) - primary contact
- Legal/Privacy team
- CEO/Board (for major matters)

##### **ISO Certification Bodies**

**Examples**: BSI Group, DNV, TÜV Rheinland, SGS, Bureau Veritas, LRQA

**What They Do**:
- Conduct certification audits for ISO standards
- Issue certificates upon successful audit
- Perform annual surveillance audits
- Conduct recertification audits every 3 years
- Investigate complaints about certified organizations
- Suspend or withdraw certificates for non-compliance

**Engagement Timeline**:
- **Application**: Submit scope and documentation
- **Stage 1 Audit** (Document Review): 1-2 days, remote or on-site
- **Gap Remediation**: 2-4 weeks if major findings
- **Stage 2 Audit** (Implementation): 2-5 days on-site (depends on scope)
- **Certificate Issuance**: 2-4 weeks after successful Stage 2
- **Surveillance Audit 1**: 12 months after certification
- **Surveillance Audit 2**: 24 months after certification
- **Recertification Audit**: 36 months after certification

**Audit Duration by Organization Size** (ISO 27001 example):
- <15 employees: 1-2 days
- 15-50 employees: 2-3 days
- 50-250 employees: 3-5 days
- 250-1000 employees: 5-8 days
- 1000+ employees: 8-15+ days

**What They Need**:
- Management system documentation (scope, policy, procedures)
- Risk assessments and treatment plans
- Evidence of control implementation
- Internal audit reports
- Management review records
- Records of competency and training
- Corrective action records

**Certification Costs**:
- Application/registration: $500-$2,000
- Stage 1 audit: $3,000-$8,000
- Stage 2 audit: $8,000-$30,000
- Certificate issuance: $1,000-$3,000
- Annual surveillance: $3,000-$10,000
- Recertification: Similar to initial certification
- *Costs vary by scope, organization size, and certification body*

**Key Contacts**:
- Quality Manager or ISMS Owner
- Compliance Officer
- Auditee coordinator (schedules interviews, provides evidence)

#### **Third-Party Vendors & Service Providers**

**Primary Role**: External organizations that process data or provide services requiring compliance

**Categories**:

##### **Cloud Service Providers** (AWS, Azure, GCP, etc.)

**Compliance Relevance**:
- Infrastructure controls (ISO 27001, SOC 2)
- Data residency and sovereignty (GDPR)
- Shared responsibility model
- BAAs for HIPAA workloads

**What You Need From Them**:
- SOC 2 Type II reports
- ISO 27001/27017/27018 certificates
- Attestations (HIPAA, GDPR compliance statements)
- Security documentation
- Incident notification procedures
- Data Processing Agreements (DPA)
- Business Associate Agreements (BAA) if handling PHI

**Typical Review Cycle**:
- Initial due diligence: 2-4 weeks
- Annual reassessment: 1-2 weeks
- Continuous monitoring: Automated tools, quarterly reviews

---

##### **SaaS Vendors** (CRM, HR systems, marketing tools, etc.)

**Compliance Relevance**:
- HIPAA Business Associates (if handling PHI)
- GDPR Data Processors
- SOC 2 for security assurance

**Due Diligence Requirements**:
- Security questionnaires (SIG, CAIQ, custom)
- SOC 2 Type II reports (annual)
- Penetration test results
- Incident response procedures
- Data Processing Agreements
- Business Associate Agreements (if applicable)
- Insurance (cyber liability, E&O)

**Vendor Risk Assessment**:
- **High Risk**: Systems with PHI, PII, financial data, mission-critical
- **Medium Risk**: Systems with business data, moderate access
- **Low Risk**: Public-facing tools, minimal data, easily replaceable

**Assessment Frequency**:
- High Risk: Annually
- Medium Risk: Every 2 years
- Low Risk: Every 3 years or upon renewal

**Red Flags**:
- No SOC 2 or equivalent certification
- Vague security responses
- No incident notification process
- Unwilling to sign BAA/DPA
- No encryption in transit/at rest
- Poor access control capabilities

##### **Consultants & Implementation Partners**

**Types**:
- Compliance consultants (guide implementation)
- Technical implementation (configure systems)
- Legal counsel (interpret regulations)
- Training providers

**Engagement Models**:
- **Project-based**: Fixed scope, deliverables, timeline
- **Retainer**: Ongoing advisory, set hours per month
- **Hourly**: Ad-hoc support

**Typical Costs**:
- Compliance consultants: $150-$400/hour
- Legal counsel: $300-$800/hour
- Technical implementation: $100-$250/hour
- Training development: $5,000-$50,000 per program

**What to Expect**:
- Gap assessments and roadmaps
- Policy and procedure templates
- Control implementation guidance
- Pre-audit readiness assessments
- Audit support (evidence preparation, interview prep)
- Remediation guidance

**Engagement Timeline**:
- **Discovery/Assessment**: 2-4 weeks
- **Implementation Guidance**: 3-6 months
- **Audit Preparation**: 4-8 weeks before audit
- **Post-Audit Support**: 2-4 weeks after audit

##### **Managed Service Providers (MSPs)**

**Services**:
- Managed security (SIEM, SOC, vulnerability management)
- IT infrastructure management
- Cloud operations
- Backup and disaster recovery

**Compliance Implications**:
- MSPs may be Business Associates (HIPAA) or Data Processors (GDPR)
- Need to demonstrate their own compliance
- Must flow down compliance obligations to MSP

**Key Requirements**:
- MSP must have SOC 2 Type II
- Right to audit clauses in contracts
- Defined SLAs for security and availability
- Incident notification timelines (24-48 hours typical)
- Data handling and retention policies

#### **Customers as Stakeholders**

**Why They Matter**:
- B2B customers often require compliance proof before purchase
- Compliance certifications reduce sales cycle friction
- Customer audits may be required (especially for enterprise sales)

**Common Customer Requirements**:
- SOC 2 Type II report
- ISO 27001 certificate
- HIPAA BAA (if handling PHI)
- GDPR DPA
- Security questionnaire responses (VSA, SIG, CAIQ)
- Penetration test results (annual)
- Right to audit (negotiate carefully)

**Customer Audit Process**:
- **Request**: Customer submits audit request
- **Scope Negotiation**: Define what they can review (2-4 weeks)
- **Information Sharing**: Provide documentation, evidence
- **On-Site Visit** (sometimes): Facility tour, interviews (1-2 days)
- **Remediation**: Address any findings (2-4 weeks)
- **Approval**: Customer approves, contract proceeds

**How to Manage**:
- Maintain compliance artifact repository (shared drive with reports, certificates, policies)
- Standardize responses (security questionnaire templates)
- Use trust centers or vendor portals (automate sharing)
- Negotiate "right to audit" vs. "right to receive audit reports" (less disruptive)

#### **Insurance Providers (Cyber Liability)**

**Why They Matter**:
- Cyber insurance often requires compliance measures
- May reduce premiums if certified (SOC 2, ISO 27001)
- Claims may require proof of compliance

**Application Process**:
- Security questionnaire (detailed)
- Evidence of controls (policies, training records)
- Incident history (breaches, near-misses)
- Compliance certifications (SOC 2, ISO 27001)
- Coverage quotes (2-4 weeks)

**Typical Requirements**:
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) mandatory
- Endpoint detection and response (EDR)
- Regular backups with offline copies
- Incident response plan
- Security awareness training
- Vulnerability management

**Claims Process**:
- Report incident immediately (24-48 hours)
- Provide evidence of controls
- May require forensic investigation
- Compliance gaps may reduce or deny claims

---

#### **Legal Counsel**

**Types**:
- **In-House Counsel**: Ongoing legal advice
- **External Counsel**: Specialized compliance expertise
- **Regulatory Counsel**: FDA, healthcare, privacy specialists

**When to Engage**:
- Regulatory interpretation (ambiguous requirements)
- Contract negotiations (BAAs, DPAs, vendor agreements)
- Regulatory submissions (FDA premarket)
- Enforcement actions or investigations
- Breach response
- International expansion (cross-border data transfers)

**Typical Engagements**:
- Policy review and approval
- Regulatory strategy
- Risk assessments (legal perspective)
- Training on legal obligations
- Regulatory correspondence

**Costs**:
- In-house: Salary + benefits
- External: $300-$800+/hour depending on firm and specialization
